Masters Internship

Simulation of magneto-thermoelectric hydrodynamics

Read Thesis in English (Chapter abstracts also in French)

Magneto-thermoelectric forces arise from the interaction between a superimposed magnetic field and electric currents generated by thermoelectric phenomena near a solid-liquid interface at a given temperature gradient (Thomson-Seebeck effect). These forces cause grain movements during directional solidification.


The EPM research group (Grenoble Alpes University) focused on comprehending these forces through simulation. We worked with EPM's internal code, Aequatio, solving partial differential equations based on a multi-mesh finite element method—highly convenient for the broad scale span in solidification.


We modeled the Navier-Stokes equations coupled with magneto-thermoelectric force, the continuity equation for electric currents, and heat transfer. Simulations were based on an Al-10%wtCu liquid domain with a single micrometric grain in a 20K/cm thermal gradient and a 0.08T static magnetic field.

CALPHAD Description of the Super-cooled Liquid

Read Thesis in Portuguese

This work used the Thermo-Calc software to model, via the CALPHAD method, the super-cooled liquid (SCL) that leads to the formation of metallic vitreous materials from the Mo-Nb-B System using the 2-State Model.


This research improved the database for thermodynamic calculations of SCLs in the computational study of materials, specifically subsystems of the Fe-Mo-Cr-Nb-B system. Its application as a metallic glass coating combines exceptional hardness, corrosion resistance, and wear resistance.


Continuing the work of the LHM research group (UFSCar), this work describes liquid molybdenum through the 2-state phenomenological model, as well as the binary liquids Mo-Nb and Mo-B.

Survey on Numerical Simulations for Hydrogen Storage in Hydrides

Read Survey in Portuguese

This study reviews the literature written to that date on the topic of hydrogen storage in metal hydrides and proposes numerical simulation projects.


The goal is to understand and model complex and multiphysics characteristics by coupling experimental data with numerical approximations of existing physical models. This bridges the gap between theoretical capacity and practical application in renewable energy infrastructure.

Hydrogen Absorption in MgH2 Composites

Read Reports in Portuguese

This study analyzed a hydrogen storage material coated with a thin film of palladium. Mg-based nanocomposites are the most studied materials for hydrogen storage in the hydride phase (MgH2) due to its high gravimetric capacity.


However, such composites are highly reactive, forming oxides and hydroxides that make handling in air impossible and reduce both kinetic efficiency and storage capacity.


The palladium surface coating aims to prevent oxidation and water formation on the surface while maintaining the material's necessary permeability to hydrogen gas.

Academic Background

PhD in Materials Science & Eng.

Universidade Federal de São Carlos (UFSCar)

2021 - Present

Specialization: Metallic Materials & Computational Chemistry.


Currently researching Hydrogen storage on High Entropy Alloys (HEA) through Density Functional Theory simulations. The advent of HEA concept opened a pathway for exploring huge compositional spaces for discovering and designing alloys with optimized performance for each specific application. This is particularly interesting for investigating alloys for Hydrogen storage, since these properties of an alloy are strongly dependent on its composition. In this project, theoretical studies will be compared with experimental data and applied to a new thermodynamic model to increase our fundamental understanding of the underlying mechanisms controlling H storage in HEAs. The scientific objective is to make the correlation between electronic/atomic structures and the thermodynamics of the hydrogen absorption/desorption since a high H storage is sought with this reversible transformation between alloy and hydride at low temperature and pressure.
